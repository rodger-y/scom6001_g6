2. Stinky tofu

It is a fermented tofu product commonly served deep-fried, enjoyed as a delicacy in China in both street settings and high-end restaurants. ₁ ₂
The name comes from its pungent smell due to fermentation; it is not inherently a sign of danger. However, the cooking process may release unpleasant odour, especially in public areas. ₁ ₂
It contains the cultural and nutritional benefits associated with tofu and fermented foods. However, it contains soy allergen, and fermentation safety varies. ₂ ₃
Tofu dishes have appeared in serious poisoning investigations, although this appears to be an occasional rather than routine risk. ₄
