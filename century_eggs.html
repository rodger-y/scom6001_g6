Century eggs

Century eggs result from a unique preservation method of duck eggs in China. 1 2

The colour is dark, and the egg white turns black and jelly-like. Some egg whites develop pine needle or snowflake patterns during the process, and are therefore also called pine-flower eggs. 1 3

Scents of sulphur and/or ammonia are expected. 1 3

They are rich in protein, and commonly served as a cold dish or entree; they are particularly delicious when paired with vinegar. That said, they have a basic (alkaline) taste on their own due to the preservation methods. 1 2

Old techniques used lead (Pb) in production; however, modern methods are not supposed to contain Pb. They are not inherently dangerous, but have been reported to contain high sodium, and egg is an allergen for some people. 2 4 5
